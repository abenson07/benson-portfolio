# FlexCard
**Category:** Product Design — Employer: Dave

---

## Case Study

By 2023, buy-now-pay-later had become a mainstream financial behavior — but it largely existed outside of banking relationships. Dave saw an opportunity to bring that model into its own ecosystem, combining it with its core cash advance product to give users the ability to split purchases into four payments and cover things they couldn't afford outright.

The primary focus was on people facing genuine emergencies or immediate needs, though the product naturally extended to discretionary purchases as well. The goal was also to deepen adoption of Dave's banking product by creating a reason to use it for everyday spending.

I led the initial concept and product strategy exploration, working directly with product managers to vet the direction and shape the early design. I handed the project off to another designer before leaving Dave. The product has since launched.

## Raw Notes (verbatim)

During my time at Dave, I was the lead designer on a number of projects, leading the initial exploration, concept design, and product strategy concepts with product managers and just kind of initially vetting the work. This was the last big project I worked on, combining both the core Dave product, which is a cash advance loan, with the buy now pay later products that were becoming more prevalent in 2023-24. The idea was partly to build up adoption of the bank product as well, allowing purchases that you made to be spared into payments of four, to allow for you to buy things that you couldn't afford at the time. The main focus was on providing results for people who needed emergencies or needs but of course it could also go out to people who had wants. So I led the initial product design of this work and then passed it off to another designer before I left the company and the product has just recently launched.
