# Dave+ Positioning
**Category:** Strategy & Research — Employer: Dave

---

## Case Study

Dave had been free to use since its launch, with a $1/month fee that only applied when someone used the credit service. The model was starting to create friction: the charge was confusing in terms of when and why it applied, and it didn't reflect the growing value Dave was delivering — or the expanded product the company wanted to build.

I led the design thinking on introducing a new subscription tier structure, with a foundational push to preserve a free tier. The paid tiers would be built around value beyond the existing credit service — not a rebrand of what already existed. To get there, I ran a series of workshops with leadership and key stakeholders to surface ideas, then structured the output around a core thesis: the tiers should level users up financially. If someone was in a stronger financial position, they'd naturally benefit more from better financial tools — and that progression would justify spending more with Dave, rather than requiring users to pay their way in.

The work was exploratory, meant to inform leadership rather than ship directly. I worked in close partnership with PM Alia and mentored a junior designer through the process. I was actively involved in leadership conversations and built out the materials they needed to evaluate the direction.

Leadership put the project on hold while assessing broader company financials — a timing decision, not a rejection of the approach.

## Raw Notes (verbatim)

At the time that I started working on this project, Dave had been a free app. You would pay $1 per month if you were using their credit service but otherwise it was free to use. The company wanted to move away from this because it was a little confusing when and why you would get charged $1. It also didn't really reflect the value that was being provided and the company was wanting to add a lot more to the product. I was in charge of leading the design thinking on introducing new subscription tiers and pushed fundamentally to allow for a free tier. The paid tiers would be things beyond the credit service that you already paid a fee on. And I ran several workshops with both leadership and key stakeholders to gather a bunch of different ideas. And then what I did was construct a series of products in such a way that leveled you up financially so that you were in a better financial position and would benefit from better financial tools, which would justify you spending more with us. This was an exploratory piece of work to inform leadership. Leadership ultimately did not move forward with this approach for a number of reasons, mainly because they put the project on hold while they assessed financials. I was involved with leadership talks and building out the necessary items for leadership to review. I worked in close concert with my PM partner Aliyah and a more junior designer that I was mentoring at the time.
