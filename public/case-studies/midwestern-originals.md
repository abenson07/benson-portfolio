## Raw Notes (verbatim)

I would say that I've always been a little bit of a builder and when I started working in the agencies I felt a little bit of an itch to keep exploring. Partnering with a couple of my engineering friends, we started doing side projects for clients and people that we knew. Along the way we found that it was necessary to have a company, a legal entity to do this under, so we founded Midwestern Originals, which was the name I came up with. We did projects for people. Over the years it became a way for us to funnel our creative ideas through. It has been everything from a digital development agency to a clothing design studio. It housed Cocktail Club, Kansas City Cocktail Club, which was our much-beloved cocktail trade teaching class that sold out across Kansas City and ultimately became predominantly a Webflow development company. We were the first Webflow experts in the Midwest and probably the most important thing about it is that it was, from its beginning, imagined and operated as a cooperative where anyone who wanted to learn how to do this work or operate under some kind of structure so they could try this type of freelancing out was welcome to join us. We've shepherded eight or more people, partnering with them as contractors to let them do the work they love to do and get paid. Famously we don't want to get rich off other people. We take a minimal cut to keep our expenses covered and pass the rest of the money on to these people and we reassess it every quarter to a year. We also use it to do other creative endeavors that we focus on. For example AI, vibe code building, and AI apps that we launch.
