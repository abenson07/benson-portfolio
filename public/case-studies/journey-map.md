# Journey Map
**Category:** Strategy & Research — Employer: Dave

---

## Case Study

Dave positioned itself as a mission-driven company. Leadership asked us to build a customer journey map to actually understand the people behind that mission and design around them more deliberately. Early into the research, the real problem surfaced: most internal stakeholders couldn't empathize with what Dave's customers were navigating — a serious gap for a product built for people who are most financially at risk.

Over several months, I partnered with a senior researcher and senior designer on conversations with 22 customers across a range of financial backgrounds. A clear pattern emerged: people felt trapped in a loop — borrow, spend, get paid, repay, land right back where they started — while actively wanting out of it. We built the synthesis around a subway-map metaphor: two people standing at the same stop could have boarded from completely different starting points, and were likely headed toward different ones too. That let us map two distinct customer backgrounds onto the same loop, and the different paths beyond it. Rather than spotlight individual stories, we blended the research into two composite characters and narrated their journeys end to end, using real recorded customer voices throughout.

Presented as an interactive, narrated map at a company all-hands, it gave leadership clear direction on where to invest in the product — and built genuine empathy across the org for what customers were actually living through.

## Raw Notes (verbatim)

Okay so this one: Dave was pitched as a mission-driven company. One of the things we were asked by leadership to do was to research and design together to develop a customer journey map to better understand our users so we can design products around them better.

As we set out to do this, we realized a couple of things:
1. The financial journey informed their journey with us.
2. The journey outside of our app, what led them to using our app and what they do after our app, had a foundational experience, part of why they used our app and why they came back. Understanding that was key.
3. As we talked to internal stakeholders, we realized that the majority of these stakeholders could not empathize with our customer or truly understand what they were going through. For a product that is targeted towards people who are most at risk financially, that was both something that was upsetting but also a big gap in having them understand the work that we were going to put together for them.

Over several months we had many conversations with 22 customers ranging from a number of different backgrounds and financial situations, etc. Working with another senior designer and senior researcher, we developed a visual metaphor of a subway map where there are stops along the journey. There are loops you end up in and there is a financial path they want to escape the loop and move forward on. This recognized the fundamental takeaway from our conversations, which was that people felt like they were in a loop. They were stuck using our product but did not want to continue using this product and wanted to get out. We first understood their background, where they were coming from. We understood that this financial education was a key issue in terms of the initial experience growing up but that education was not the thing that was going to solve this. Now we identified that there were two key distinct backgrounds people came from:
1. People who were financially secure growing up but then got in over their heads when they became adults and couldn't get out and fell behind.
2. People who were not financially secure growing up and did not face the same kind of prospects that others did but also stayed behind.

And then we understood the different ways why people came to need a tool like Dave. We also understood the journey through:
- getting the advance
- using it
- getting paid
- paying the advance back
- and then ending up right back where they were because they're behind still

We understood where they wanted to go. Dave had a lot of other products that they couldn't get people to use, specifically a bank. We asked them what they enjoyed about their current bank and we asked them questions about their current experience.

Then we asked them:
- why they wanted to be one of them
- what they wanted their financials to be
- what they wanted to have happen
- how they imagined getting there

All those things. We identified that there were some people who did achieve that. What we understood was that there was a key period of getting ahead that followed the financial journey that our product had shared with us, which was borrow, spend, save, and grow. A lot of our users were stuck in borrow. They struggled. They got into the spend phase when they got paid but then they could not get to the save portion because of the challenges they had with spending.

This unlocked a whole set of realizations for us about where we should focus our product strategy and where we should invest in our users. We chose not to highlight one or two of their stories but to blend their stories together and tell a narrative of two individuals representing the two different backgrounds and telling their story through this path. We imagined a bright future for them and showed a path forward in this journey. We took excerpts from their stories, their recordings, and put their real words and real voices into the presentation. We made an interactive map and a presentation that narrated the journey from start to end with their voices included and shared it at a company all hands and used it to influence people and more importantly to build empathy.
